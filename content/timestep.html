Further Investigations (Feb. 2013) ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- It appears that there are still problems with multistepping. In the perturbed disk slice IC, I am getting (very) large energy non-conservation. As the following plots show, I am getting a handful of particles that are (much) hotter than the maximum feedback temperature ($1.8 * 10^8 K$). [](Research/dSteps_toohot_bug/phase_46Myr.png) [](Research/dSteps_toohot_bug/temperature_evolution.png) This isn't because of a feedback problem: [](Research/dSteps_toohot_bug/hotparticle_uDotFB.png) Once again, this problem goes away when singlestepping is used: [](Research/dSteps_toohot_bug/bad_multistepping_temp.png) [](Research/dSteps_toohot_bug/bad_multistepping_energy.png) This is ultimately causing a crash when particle 63340 gets a massive uDotPdV applied to it. The difference in the phase diagrams of the single and multi-stepping runs is *very* scary: [](Research/dSteps_toohot_bug/phase_46Myr_badstepping.png) [](Research/dSteps_toohot_bug/phase_46Myr_1step.png) [](Research/dSteps_toohot_bug/phase_46Myr_multistep.png) {% endblock main %}
